    Report:

    This video demonstrates the Duckiebot's ability to drive in a straight line for a distance of 2 meters.   • A video of Duckiebot running the lane following demo

    Report:

    This video shows the Duckiebot completing 2 rounds of lane-following demo, where it follows the road on auto-pilot. Often times, the robot had difficulty staying in the lane, especially at turns. This could be due to small errors in camera calibration, delays in motor response, or difficulty detecting the lane in certain lighting conditions. This highlights the importance of proper wheels and camera calibrations.

    The Duckiebot follows the lane by using its camera to see the road and find the lane markings, like the yellow line in the center or the white lines on the sides. It looks at where it is on the road and adjusts its direction if it starts drifting off the lane. The robot keeps checking and making small movements to stay in the lane.

  • A screen capture of intrinsics calibration.yaml file

    Report:

    The intrinsics calibration is essential for correcting camera distortions, ensuring that the camera accurately perceives the real world. This calibration process determines the camera's internal parameters, such as focal length and lens distortion coefficients. We performed the intrinsics calibration by moving the camera in various angles and distances capturing checkerboard pattern from different prespectives. This ensures that the calibration accurately accounts for the camera's field of view and distortion.Accurate intrinsics calibration is important for reliable lane detection and object recognition in robotics.

  • A screen capture of extrinsics calibration.yaml file

    Report:

    The extrinsics calibration ensures the robot understands the camera’s position and angle relative to its body. This is essential for accurate lane-following and object detection, as it allows the robot to interpret what the camera sees in relation to its surroundings.
